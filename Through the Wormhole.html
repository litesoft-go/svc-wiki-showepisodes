Through the Wormhole is an American science documentary television series narrated and hosted by American actor Morgan Freeman. It began airing on Science in the United States on June 9, 2010.[1]

Development and production[edit]

The TV network Science has been highlighting what VP of Production Bernadette McDaid calls the "Rock Stars of Science",[2] and physics outreach such as Michio Kaku and Brian Cox. "We wanted to merge our 'Rock Stars of Science' ... with the superstars of pop culture."[2] When Science general manager Deborah Myers heard that Morgan Freeman was very interested in things to do with the universe and space and "asks the big philosophical questions",[2] she approached Freeman and his producer and proposed making a series together.[2]

On February 17, 2011, Sean Carroll confirmed on his Twitter page that filming of season 2 of Through the Wormhole began.[3] On May 17, 2011, Discovery confirmed the second season would premiere on Science on June 8, 2011.[4] An episode from the second season was supposed to air on July 13, 2011,[4] but went unaired. It was later released on the season 2 DVD as the sixth episode.[5]

On January 3, 2012, Sean Carroll posted a picture on his Twitter page, mentioning that it was taken during the taping of season 3.[6] Season 3 began with a special episode on March 6, 2012,[7] and the remaining nine episodes began airing on June 6, 2012.[8]

Season 4 of Through the Wormhole began with a special episode on March 20, 2013,[9] and the remaining nine episodes began airing on June 5, 2013.[10]

On October 9, 2013, the Science Channel began airing enhanced episodes of the show under the title Beyond the Wormhole with Morgan Freeman.[11]

Season 5 of Through the Wormhole began with a special episode on March 5, 2014,[12] and the remaining nine episodes began airing on June 4, 2014.[13]

On March 10, 2014, series producer Anthony Lund stated in an interview with the Los Angeles Post-Examiner that "Wormhole season 6 is a GO, and I’m dreaming of new, thought provoking ideas to explore with this show."[14]

Season 6 of Through the Wormhole premiered on April 29, 2015.[15] Season 6 consists of six episodes, unlike the previous seasons, which all have ten (except season 1, which has 8 episodes).[16]

On March 31, 2016, Science Channel announced it would return for a seventh season,[17] which premiered on August 30, 2016.
