Halt and Catch Fire is an American period drama television series created by Christopher Cantwell and Christopher C. Rogers, that premiered on AMC on June 1, 2014.[1][2] The series depicts a fictionalized insider's view of the personal computer revolution of the early 1980s. The first two seasons of the series is set in the Silicon Prairie of Texas while the third season moves the setting to Silicon Valley.[3][4] The show's title refers to computer machine code instruction HCF, whose execution would cause the computer's central processing unit to stop working (but not really catch fire).[5]

The series was renewed for a 10-episode third season,[6] which had a sneak peek premiere on August 21, 2016,[7] and its official two-hour premiere on August 23, 2016.[8] As of September 20, 2016[update], 26 episodes of Halt and Catch Fire have aired.

Joe MacMillan, a key player in the debut of the IBM Personal Computer, questions college students about their knowledge of several computing categories. Joe came to Cardiff to recruit Gordon Clark, a sales engineer and former system builder, into reverse engineering an IBM PC with him. Gordon is initially reluctant to do the project, telling Joe that he has a family to consider; Gordon had also suffered a public humiliation when the Symphonic, a microcomputer he and his wife Donna created, failed to boot at COMDEX in 1981. However, Gordon reconsiders Joe's proposal and is able to reconstruct the assembly language code of the IBM PC BIOS. Bosworth and company owner Nathan Cardiff confront Joe and Gordon, informing them that IBM knows about their renegade project, and that Cardiff Electric is being sued for copyright infringement. Cameron eventually decides to implement Swap Meet's changes; however, she tells Donna that she wants to fire Doug and Craig. When Donna tells Diane of Cameron's intentions, Diane states that she would reluctantly accept Cameron's decision if it would benefit the company; however, Donna lies to Cameron, saying that Diane won't let her fire Doug and Craig. MacMillan Utility loses a major contract with General Atomics, which represented 37 percent of their revenue, after Joe is insulted by a senior executive during a meeting. Despite this, Joe gives Ryan access credentials to ARPANET, and Joe tells Ryan to map the network before their security clearance is revoked.

At Mutiny, the coders discover that users want a way to make cash transactions on Swap Meet; Doug and Craig have been working on a solution to accept credit card transactions, and Donna implores Cameron it implement it, but she is reluctant to do so. At Joe's apartment, while studying the ARPANET map, Joe sees potential in NSFNET; however, Ryan sees it as a waste of time, since commercial usage of the network is illegal. During the Fourth of July weekend, Bosworth and Cameron return to Dallas, with Bosworth seeing his newborn grandson, and Cameron intending to retrieve her father's motorcycle, as her mother and stepfather are moving to Florida; she also meets with her ex-boyfriend Tom. When her father's motorcycle is sold to someone else, Bosworth and Cameron have an argument, and he returns to San Francisco without her. Donna gives Doug and Craig the green light to implement their credit card interface; however, when Cameron finally returns to Mutiny, she and Donna argue over that decision. Joe stands by his decision to focus on NSFNET, telling Ryan that they're going to create a regional network from it. When Cameron calls Diane, Cameron finds out about Donna's lie.
26 6 "And She Was" Michael Morris Angelina Burnett September 20, 2016 (2016-09-20) 0.280[35]
At MacMillan Utility, Joe and Ryan begin setting up their regional network. At Mutiny, Cameron reveals that she just fired Doug and Craig; Diane then comes into the conference room, telling Cameron and Donna that they have received an acquisition offer from CompuServe, and that Mutiny should consider going public. Sensing tension between them, Diane tells Cameron and Donna to think about the decision over the weekend, inviting them to her vacation house in Sonoma to unwind. While Donna takes up Diane's offer, Cameron stays at home, where she bonds with Gordon while playing Super Mario Bros. and working with Gordon's amateur radio; while bonding, Gordon reveals his condition to Cameron and airs his grievances with Joe. Bosworth goes to the opera, and he finds Diane there, and the two later meet at a bar. At night, Cameron goes to Joe’s apartment to convince him to admit that he had stolen Gordon’s idea. Cameron later unexpectedly moves out of the Clarks’ house, and she reveals to Gordon that she and Tom had gotten married. Ken Diebold, a senior executive at MacMillan Utility, informs Joe that the board has rejected his NSFNET proposal, and Ken revokes Joe’s executive authority at the company. During a deposition meeting, Joe admits that he stole Gordon’s idea.
